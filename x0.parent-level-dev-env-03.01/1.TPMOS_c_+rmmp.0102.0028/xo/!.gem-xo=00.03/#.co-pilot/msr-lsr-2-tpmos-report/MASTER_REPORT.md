# WSR Project Master Report: State of the Simulation

## Executive Summary
Martian Street Race (WSR) is an ambitious terminal-based simulation of a dual-planet economy. It features a robust multi-process architecture that separates the UI, the clock, and the economic scheduler. While the foundation for entity management (governments and corporations) and data persistence (the Master Ledger) is exceptionally strong, the core financial cycles that drive the economy (taxes, payroll, dividends) remain in a placeholder state.

## Key Findings

### 1. Architectural Brilliance
The use of an orchestrator (`m.button...`) to manage concurrent processes for UI, Clock, and Scheduler is a high-level design choice that ensures the game feels "alive" even when the player is idle.

### 2. The Ledger is Truth
Everything in the game stems from `master_ledger.txt`. The implementation of `master_reader.c` as a universal parsing tool is the right approach for maintaining a "single source of truth."

### 3. The "Missing Heart"
The game currently lacks the "metabolism" of a real economy. Money is generated via the analysis loop (price changes) and player startups, but it doesn't yet flow through the systemic loops of taxes, wages, and profit distribution.

## Strategy for Completion

### Immediate Priority: "The Metabolism"
The most critical next step is to transform `tax_loop.c`, `payroll_loop.c`, and `dividend_loop.c` from empty shells into active participants in the economy. This will provide the necessary "sink" for the capital generated by the `analysis_loop.c`.

### Secondary Priority: "Narrative & Reaction"
Connecting the `news_loop.c` to the `analysis_loop.c` will create the feedback loop that makes the market feel reactive and immersive.

## Final Assessment
The project is approximately **60% complete**. The "heavy lifting" of the engine is done. The remaining 40% involves implementing the specific logic for economic cycles and refining the player's interface for managing their empire.
