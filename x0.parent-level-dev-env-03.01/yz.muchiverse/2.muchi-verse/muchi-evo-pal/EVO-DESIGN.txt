================================================================================
0. WHAT GROUNDS THIS
================================================================================
- **E.V.O.: Search for Eden** (Enix, SNES, 1992) - real, well-known
  mechanics, described here at the level of general public knowledge
  (specific numeric costs/stage names not claimed with false
  precision): player controls one creature lineage, beginning as a
  simple aquatic organism. Eating other creatures/organic matter earns
  Evolution Points (EP). EP are spent (via a guiding "Gaia" entity)
  on new body parts - fins, jaws, legs, wings, shells, horns, etc. -
  each with real stat effects (attack/defense/speed/etc.) and usually a
  visible body change. Certain parts gate access to new areas/eras
  (e.g., legs needed to leave water for land). Progresses across
  geological eras toward increasingly complex forms, ending near
  human/proto-sapient form. A rival, competing creature lineage evolves
  in parallel and recurs as an antagonist across eras.
- **SimEarth** (Maxis, SNES port 1993) - real, well-known mechanics:
  a god-game/planetary simulation - player manages global systems
  (atmosphere composition, temperature, tectonics/continents, ocean
  coverage) across geological time, with life evolving through
  simplified ages (single-celled life, plants, invertebrates,
  vertebrates, up through an Age of Man and beyond). Multiple species
  exist simultaneously with tracked population/biomass, competing for
  niches, subject to player-triggered or emergent events
  (terraforming, disasters, extinctions) and feedback-loop dynamics
  (a simplified Gaia-hypothesis model).
- `wrai-dust-fable` §2/§14 - Dustopia's own "size=time" scaling and
  "fractal zoom applies to AI compute, not just pixels" framing -
  directly what makes "two games at two scales" a coherent single
  system rather than a hack.
- `COMPOSABLE-MATERIALS-ARCHITECTURE.md` §2's composable data shape
  (`granularity`/`lambda`/`bond_profile`/`composed_of`/`decision_mode`)
  - reused wholesale below, not redesigned. §3's worked example already
  names atom->molecule->cell->organism->structure->environment; this
  document proposes ONE addition to that chain (§2 below).
- `piececraft-wraith`'s proven key-`8` 2D-top-down/3D-perspective
  toggle (`GRAND-ARCHITECTURE.md` §0) - the real, working precedent for
  "one scene descriptor, switchable zoom/view," reused here as the
  mechanism for switching between the planetary and creature views
  (§4 below).
- `wsr-pal`'s real `presets/schedule.txt` interval-scheduler
  (`wsr-pal/dox/00-overview.md`, read directly this session) - reused
  here for geological-era/climate ticking (§2 below).
- `MUCHICIVO_BIBLE.txt` §2/§3 - the Tier1->Tier2 graduation condition
  (`decision_mode` crossing `rl`->`llm`) and the 4-tier chassis - this
  document is where that definition gets a concrete, playable moment
  (§6 below).

================================================================================
1. THE SYNTHESIS: TWO NESTED SCALES, ONE SYSTEM
================================================================================
```
ZOOMED OUT (SimEarth-flavored)          ZOOMED IN (E.V.O.-flavored)
"ecosystem/planet" tier                  "organism/lineage" tier
  - global climate/terrain/era state       - one creature lineage's body,
  - population/biomass per species           built as a composable chain
    (aggregate, not per-individual)         - EP economy, body-part
  - mostly PRESET/deterministic               acquisition (craft.c reuse)
    simulation (like Dustopia's own          - decision_mode lives HERE,
    bond-table chemistry - see §5)             not at the planetary layer
  - sets the CONSTRAINTS/OPPORTUNITIES     - responds to what the
    the creature layer plays within           planetary layer currently
    (is there land yet? what's the            allows/rewards
    climate, what niches are open)
```
Neither layer is optional or secondary - SimEarth's layer is WHY the
world changes under the player's feet across eras (the actual reason
"Cambrian ocean" becomes "prehistoric land" becomes whatever's next);
E.V.O.'s layer is WHERE the player (and rival lineages) actually play
and make choices. This is not a compromise between two designs, it's
recognizing they were always answering different questions.

================================================================================
2. THE ECOSYSTEM/PLANETARY LAYER (SimEarth-flavored)
================================================================================
**New granularity tier, added to `COMPOSABLE-MATERIALS-ARCHITECTURE.md`
§2's chain, motivated directly by needing somewhere to put SimEarth's
mechanics**: `granularity=ecosystem`, sitting between `organism` and
`structure` in that document's existing ladder. An ecosystem composable
aggregates:
  - `composed_of=<species_lineage_id>:<population_count>, ...` - reuses
    the SAME `composed_of` mechanism yet again (now: crafting recipes,
    tech trees per `MUCHICIVO_BIBLE.txt` §4, AND ecosystem population -
    three applications of one mechanism, a real running theme worth
    naming explicitly across all three documents).
  - `bond_profile`-driven climate/terrain state - Dustopia's 0/1/Z/3
    table applied to atmosphere/temperature/terrain composables (per
    `COMPOSABLE-MATERIALS-ARCHITECTURE.md` §3's own robot/environment
    tier note that the SAME schema covers non-organic material,
    generalized here to planetary systems).
  - `growth_limit`/`shrink_limit` (§13.2/§13.3-derived, per
    `COMPOSABLE-MATERIALS-ARCHITECTURE.md` §5) - population caps, an
    extinction condition (population hitting `shrink_limit=0`), and
    resource caps that ARE SimEarth's own carrying-capacity mechanic,
    already available from an existing field, not new to invent.

**Ticking mechanism**: `wsr-pal`'s real `presets/schedule.txt` concept
(interval -> command dispatch, `wsr-pal/dox/00-overview.md`), reused
directly - `1_era` (or a finer real-time interval mapped to in-game
eras) triggers a climate/population-update op, exactly matching that
project's own already-real "genuinely reusable piece of infrastructure"
finding.

**CORRECTED (2026-07-16, direct user correction to this document's own
first draft):** the first version of this section claimed FSM/RL
should "essentially never" apply at this layer - that was wrong, and
contradicts `wrai-dust-fable` §2 Pillar 3's own literal framing, not
just this document's later §5. Dustopia's "Fuzz Pets" are explicitly
NOT inert points - "particles aren't dead points, they're small
resonant 'creatures' sliding along a continuous spectrum" - meaning an
ATOM-granularity composable is, per Dustopia's own design, already a
pet/entity-shaped thing, not architecturally barred from carrying a
`decision_mode` field. The correct statement is: `decision_mode`
applies at EVERY granularity tier down to atoms/gravity/weather, per
§0's Dustopia citations - defaulting to `preset` at this layer for cost
reasons (`wrai-dust-fable` §14's "most agents, most of the time, should
run at the cheapest tier" - a planet's weather being cheap BY DEFAULT
is still the right call), but never architecturally forbidden from
escalating (a rare, narratively-important storm system, or an
individual "fuzz pet" atom involved in something unusual, could
legitimately run `weighted`/`rl` at this tier too). See §5 for the
corrected, fractal statement of this across the whole document.

================================================================================
3. THE CREATURE/LINEAGE LAYER (E.V.O.-flavored)
================================================================================
A creature is an `organism`-granularity composable
(`COMPOSABLE-MATERIALS-ARCHITECTURE.md` §2, unmodified), whose
`composed_of` list is literally its body parts: `composed_of=
fin:1,jaw:1` etc. **Acquiring a new body part is `craft.c`'s existing
consume/produce recipe mechanism, reused yet again**: EP is the
consumed resource (matching a `recipes.txt` row's `req1:qty1` shape,
just with an abstract "EP" resource instead of a physical material),
the new body part is the produced result. Zero new crafting engine to
write - this is the fourth cited reuse of that one mechanism across
this whole document family (crafting itself, tech trees, ecosystem
population, now creature evolution).

  - **EP economy**: earned by an `eat` op (already real and working in
    mutaclsym as `eat.c`, per its own architecture doc - reusable
    directly, not reinvented) against other organism/plant composables
    in the same ecosystem.
  - **Era gates**: certain body parts are marked as prerequisites for
    certain `ecosystem`-tier states (e.g. a `legs` part required before
    the creature can act within a `terrain=land` ecosystem composable)
    - this is where §2's planetary layer and this layer actually touch:
    the ecosystem's current state gates which body-part compositions
    are even useful/accessible, exactly mirroring E.V.O.'s own real
    design (no reason to have legs before there's land to use them on).
  - **Rival lineage**: a second organism-composable lineage, AI-
    controlled, running the SAME body-evolution loop - the natural,
    concrete first `decision_mode=weighted`/`rl` candidate in this
    whole tier (per §5).

================================================================================
4. HOW THE TWO LAYERS INTERACT - THE ZOOM CAMERA
================================================================================
One scene, camera-driven, not two separate screens with a loading
transition - directly reusing piececraft-wraith's OWN proven key-`8`
2D/3D toggle mechanism (`GRAND-ARCHITECTURE.md` §0): "zoomed out" shows
the ecosystem/planetary composable's aggregate state (population
graphs, climate, terrain map - the SimEarth view); "zoomed in" focuses
one lineage's organism composable and switches to the E.V.O.-style
body-editing/eating gameplay. This is not a new rendering mechanism to
design - it's the SAME shared presenter (`wraith_rgb_daemon.c`-style,
per `GRAND-ARCHITECTURE.md` §0's resolution) reading a different scene
descriptor depending on current zoom level, exactly the "one shared
daemon, every project gets a mirror for free" pattern already
established.

================================================================================
5. WHERE FSM/RL DROPS IN, SPECIFICALLY (direct answer, corrected
   2026-07-16 - the chassis is FRACTAL, not creature-only)
================================================================================
**Corrected statement, replacing this section's first draft**: `decision_
mode` is not exclusive to the creature/lineage layer - it applies at
EVERY granularity tier in `COMPOSABLE-MATERIALS-ARCHITECTURE.md`'s
chain, atoms and weather/gravity included, per `wrai-dust-fable` §2
Pillar 3's own "Fuzz Pets... small resonant creatures" framing (§2
above has the corrected citation). What actually varies by tier is the
DEFAULT, not the AVAILABILITY:
  - Atom/weather/gravity tier: `preset` by overwhelming default (cheap,
    matches §14's "most agents, most of the time, cheapest tier"
    framing) - but a specific storm system, a specific unusual particle
    interaction, or any other narratively-significant moment at this
    scale CAN legitimately run `weighted`/`rl` without breaking any
    architectural rule. Nothing stops it; it's just rarely worth the
    cost, exactly the same "fractal zoom" logic Dustopia already
    applies to rendering detail.
  - Ecosystem tier (§2): same story - population/climate dynamics
    default `preset`, but a specific crisis event (a mass extinction
    trigger, a first-contact-between-species event) is a legitimate
    `weighted`/`rl`/even `llm` moment.
  - Organism/creature tier (§3): the tier where escalation happens
    MOST OFTEN and most meaningfully for this game's actual player-
    facing loop (per §6's graduation moment), simply because that's
    where the interesting, high-stakes, individual decisions
    concentrate - not because other tiers are forbidden from having
    them.
This reframing matters for one concrete reason: it means the SAME
mechanism (one `decision_mode` field, one chassis) governs literally
everything in this game, at every scale, with no special-cased
exception anywhere - which is a stronger, more honest version of
`wrai-dust-fable` §12's own "one FSM chassis, four ways to choose"
claim than treating any tier as permanently exempt would have been.

Within the creature layer specifically, `MUCHICIVO_BIBLE.txt` §12's
chassis applies as a progression across ONE lineage's own eras, not a
fixed assignment (this part of the original design was correct, kept
as-is):
  - Early/simple creatures (single-celled, simple aquatic forms):
    `preset` - a handful of hardcoded eat/flee/reproduce behaviors,
    matching E.V.O.'s own earliest, simplest stages.
  - Mid-complexity creatures: `weighted` - eat/flee/mate weights tuned
    by what's actually worked (§12 Tier 2's own "nudge the weight up"
    mechanism), giving the rival lineage a genuinely responsive, non-
    scripted-feeling opponent without any model calls.
  - Complex creatures approaching the top of the era ladder: `rl` -
    full learned policy from the free `(state,action,outcome)` logging
    every FSM tick already produces.
  - **The graduation moment**: the FIRST creature in a lineage to need
    `decision_mode=llm` (genuine judgment/novelty, not just tuned
    instinct - the point where "what should I evolve into next" stops
    being answerable by a fixed weight table) IS, by
    `MUCHICIVO_BIBLE.txt` §2's own definition, the exact moment that
    lineage graduates to Tier 2 (civilization). This document makes
    that definition concrete and playable: it's the same moment E.V.O.
    itself ends (reaching near-human form) - the two framings (a
    decision_mode field crossing a threshold, and "the creature became
    human") are now literally describing the same in-game event, not
    two different milestones to reconcile.

================================================================================
6. THE GRADUATION MOMENT, PLAYED OUT
================================================================================
Concretely, what a player experiences: a lineage's creature accumulates
body parts and EP across eras (§3), climbing `decision_mode` tiers as
its behavior gets more sophisticated (§5), while the ecosystem/
planetary layer (§2) keeps changing the terrain/climate/available
niches underneath it. At some point - not a fixed turn count, an
emergent outcome of how well that lineage has actually done - the
lineage's own complexity crosses into needing `llm`-tier judgment.
That's the handoff into `muchi-civ-pal` (Tier 2): the graduated
lineage's population becomes the starting civilization, its accumulated
body-part composable chain becomes flavor/trait data for that
civilization's starting units (a species that evolved wings might start
Tier 2 with an aerial-scout unit type, for instance - not designed
further here, named as the natural place civ's own starting-conditions
design should look).

================================================================================
  1. ONE ecosystem composable + ONE organism composable, `composed_of`
     wired via `craft.c`'s existing mechanism (per §3) - prove the

  3. `decision_mode=preset` on ONE rival-lineage creature - the
     earliest, cheapest FSM tier, per §5's own ordering.
  4. The zoom-camera toggle (§4), reusing piececraft-wraith's proven
     mechanism - deliberately AFTER 1-3 work in whatever minimal view
     exists first (don't build the camera before there's two real
     layers worth toggling between).
  5. `weighted`/`rl` tiers on the rival lineage, then (only once a
     lineage has genuinely earned it via 1-4's loop actually running)
     the first real `llm`-tier graduation test (§6) - the actual proof
     this whole document's central idea works.

HUMAN-CONTROLLABLE VS. AUTOMATED - REPORT (2026-07-16)
================================================================================
Direct answer to "run it, see how it's going, human-controllable vs
automated." Before this pass, the game was 100% automated with NO
human-control path at all - `button.sh` only had `tick` (auto-runs 12
FSM ticks in one batch) and `reset`. Added a real 5th `decision_mode`
(4 = human) plus the plumbing a human-driven tick loop actually needs,
then ran all three reachable modes back to back for a genuine
comparison, not a hypothetical one.

**What had to be built for "human-controllable" to mean anything real**:
`main_loop.pal`'s 12-tick batch runs to completion inside one `prisc+x`
process with no pause point - a human can't interrupt it mid-run. Added
`pal/single_tick.pal` (same FSM dispatch, exactly ONE tick, then halt)
plus two new `button.sh` actions: `step` (run one tick) and `choose
eat|evolve` (queue a decision). `ops/bot_choose.c` gained a `decision_
mode=4` branch that does NOT auto-resolve - if no decision is queued,
it leaves the FSM parked in "deciding" and returns without acting,
confirmed by direct test (`button.sh step` with nothing queued printed
"[human] waiting for input" and left `current_state=1` unchanged,
across a real second invocation, not simulated).

**Automated tiers (preset, decision_mode=0 / llm, decision_mode=3)**:
one `./button.sh tick` command runs the full eat-eat-eat-evolve arc
(12 internal ticks) unattended. LLM tier timed at ~1.9s wall-clock for
the whole run (3 real live calls to gemma3:270m on the LAN Mac mixed in
among the trivial state-transition ticks) - fast enough that "automated
via LLM" doesn't feel meaningfully slower than pure preset for this
tiny model/tiny decision. Both automated tiers produced IDENTICAL
correct behavior (eat until ep=3, then evolve, gain "fin").

**Human tier (decision_mode=4)**: the same eat-eat-eat-evolve arc took
9 separate manual shell invocations (step/choose/step/step/step/choose/
step/step/step) instead of one `tick` command - genuinely slower to
drive, as expected, and genuinely requires a person paying attention
at each "deciding" state, not a rubber-stamp. **One real guardrail
caught during testing, not designed in advance and then verified - it
was actually exercised**: queued "evolve" while `ep=1` (below the
3-EP requirement); `bot_choose.c`'s own logic (`evolve = (decision ==
"evolve") && ep >= 3`) silently downgraded it to "eat" instead of
letting the human break the rule or crashing - the human tier is
constrained by the same rules the automated tiers are, not a bypass.

**Honest overall assessment**: the automated tiers (preset/llm) are
currently far more "playable" in the sense of actually producing a
complete creature life-cycle with one command; the human tier is real
and correctly wired but is closer to a debug/step-through harness right
now than a game a person would enjoy driving - each `step`+`choose`
pair is a raw shell command, not a UI. This is expected and appropriate
for THIS vertical slice (§7's own build order deliberately deferred the
interactive keyboard/renderer loop) - the human tier's value right now
is proving decision_mode=4's plumbing is correct and enforces the same
rules as the automated tiers, not delivering a polished play experience
yet. A real playable human mode needs the keyboard_input/renderer loop
`button.sh`'s own header comment already named as deferred future work
- `step`+`choose` are the correct underlying primitives for that UI to
eventually call, not a dead end to redo.
